About Me

I came into tech at a very young age.

For as long as I can remember, I've been fascinated with the ways technology impacts our lives. I saw this first hand in school, during college, and while working in journalism. In my life I've used technology to help people in many ways, from finding the score of last night's game to applying for a job to providing accurate severe weather alerts and everything in between.

I especially love the good that technology can do in the world, which inspired me to dive into software engineering and web development during the pandemic. Now, I want to use my skills to help marginalized people thrive. I am a proud black biracial Latinx person, as well as a proud nonbinary trans queer person (they/them pronouns, please!), so intersectionality is very important to me. In addition, I recognize that people across identities lack access to high-speed internet (or any internet at all), making tasks that many of us take for granted extremely difficult.

Over the years we have seen how technology can bring people together, and it is inspiring to me. But I believe it can also help everyone, regardless of identity or zip code, live their best life.
